Looking for your next contract? Try looking outside the capital…

  • 2 March 2017

If you are looking for a new IT contract job, it might be worth casting your eye over opportunities in Bournemouth, Liverpool, or Brighton. Those are some of the fastest-growing tech hubs in the country, according to a new report, with the first two now outpacing London in terms of jobs created.

It is fair to say that Bournemouth and Poole are starting from a much lower base, but still more than tripled, growing by 212% between 2014 and 2015. TheCityUK's Enabling Growth Across the UK report found that Liverpool had the next highest growth rate, doubling its tech-related jobs with an increase of 119%. Inner London came next, growing by 92%.

The capital starts from a far higher base of course and the 252,000 people working within technology in central London is by far the highest of any location. It accounts, in fact, for around a third of all IT and tech jobs in the UK. The next biggest tech hub in term of jobs is Bristol and Bath, which provides just under 61,700 jobs.

The findings follow Tech City’s earlier Tech Nation report from 2016, which found that 80% of tech clusters had experienced a growth in digital turnover, digital jobs, and advertised digital salaries. Southampton had the greatest growth in digital turnover during the early part of this decade, increasing by 180% between 2010 and 2014. This was followed by the hub of Truro, Redruth, and Camborne (which experienced a growth of 153%), and Dundee (129%). London came fourth with an increase in digital turnover of 101%.

You might have other considerations such as location and family issues when it comes to deciding where you work. As a flexible contractor though, it might well be worth taking a look around the UK when looking for your next contract.
